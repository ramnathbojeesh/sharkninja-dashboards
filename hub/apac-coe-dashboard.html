Executive overview

One pane of glass across seven markets, nine platforms and the full marketing-to-revenue chain. The command centre continuously answers: what happened, why, what commercial impact it created, and what to do next.

C Claude executive briefing · Q2 FY26

APAC marketing is healthy and accelerating. Revenue attributed to marketing reached US$148.2M (+12.8% QoQ) at a blended 5.4x ROI, with marketing health scoring 78/100 — up 6 points. Growth is led by ANZ and Japan, where the Ninja Creami and Ninja SLUSHi launches converted unusually strong organic advocacy into Shopify revenue.

Why it happened: the chain is consistent — a +31% lift in positive "ease of use" and "innovation" conversation 2–3 weeks ahead of sales preceded the Creami sell-through, and review ratings of 4.6★+ correlated with a +5.1pt conversion-rate uplift. Influencer-led commerce (not awareness) drove ~18% of incremental kitchen revenue.

Where to focus next: (1) protect the Shark FlexStyle story in Korea where "noise" and "price" sentiment is softening conversion; (2) scale the Creami/SLUSHi influencer model into Indonesia & Malaysia where SOV is rising but spend is under-indexed; (3) fix the Indonesia delivery-complaint cluster before it suppresses ratings. Detailed actions in Executive Recommendations.

Campaign performance & marketing ROI

Beyond engagement: Claude correlates every campaign's reach and creative with downstream Shopify revenue, ROAS and incremental sales lift.

C Claude campaign intelligence

Highest revenue: Creami "Scoop of Summer" (ANZ) — $14.8M at 7.9× ROAS; influencer-led recipe content carried 41% of conversions. Highest ROI channel: creator-commerce + owned email retargeting, not paid social broad reach.

Engaged but didn't convert: "FlexStyle Glass Hair" (Korea) generated the most engagement per dollar yet a below-average 2.1% add-to-cart rate — messaging skewed aspirational over functional; "noise" objections went unaddressed. Recommended fix: shift 20% of spend to comparison/demo creative and bundle a quiet-mode message.

C Claude consumer synthesis

What consumers love: Creami's "café dessert at home" framing and Crispi's portability; Shark Detect Pro's automatic dirt-sensing wows first-time owners. What they dislike: FlexStyle noise, Creami container cleaning friction, and beauty-device price.

Emerging needs: quieter motors, dishwasher-safe parts, and "results-proof" content for beauty. Strongest organic advocacy: Ninja Creami and Shark Detect Pro — both convert advocacy into measurable Shopify revenue.

Product attribute intelligence

C What this tells marketing

Ease of use and innovation are the highest revenue-correlated attributes (r = 0.71 / 0.66) — these are the messages to amplify. Noise and price are the biggest negative drags, concentrated on FlexStyle and beauty devices. Cleaning & maintenance is a swing attribute: positive for floorcare, a friction point for Creami.
